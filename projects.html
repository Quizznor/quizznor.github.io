digit classifier

This was my first real autodidactic project. During my Erasmus semester in Norway, with the arctic winter and COVID demanding I stay inside, I had a lot of free time on my hand that I used to teach myself about C++ and neural networks.

I ended up constructing a (working!) implementation of a dense layer from scratch in order to create arbitrary feed-forward neural networks.  Granted, my chosen architecture is a terrible choice for image recognition, but gradient descent, back propagation and loss minimization all work as they're designed to do!

orbital footprint

A fun little weekend project that I wrote to help a friend with their orbital mechanics assignment. Given a stable orbit (defined by 5 unique orbital parameters), calculate the footprint on earth. This is especially useful to intuitively understand the importance of geostationary orbits, Molniya orbits or the configuration of GPS sattelites.
